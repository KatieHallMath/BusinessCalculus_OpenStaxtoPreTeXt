By the second derivative test, we conclude that \(f\) has a local maximum at \(x=-\sqrt{3}\) and \(f\) has a local minimum at \(x=\sqrt{3}.\) The second derivative test is inconclusive at \(x=0.\) To determine whether \(f\) has a local extrema at \(x=0,\) we apply the first derivative test. To evaluate the sign of \(f'(x)=5x^2(x^2-3)\) for \(x∈(-\sqrt{3},0)\) and \(x∈(0,\sqrt{3}),\) let \(x=-1\) and \(x=1\) be the two test points. Since \(f'(-1)\lt 0\) and \(f'(1)\lt 0,\) we conclude that \(f\) is decreasing on both intervals and, therefore, \(f\) does not have a local extrema at \(x=0\) as shown in the following graph.
