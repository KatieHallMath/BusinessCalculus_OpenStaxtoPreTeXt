Solution 5.87.1.

We know the derivative of \(t^3+3t+1\) is \(3t^2+3,\) so we set \(u=t^3+3t+1.\) Then \(du=(3t^2+3)dt.\) Moreover, \(\frac{1}{3}du=(t^2+1)dt.\) Substituting into the integral, we have

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle{ \int\frac{t^2+1}{(t^3+3t+1)^4}dt=\frac{1}{3} \displaystyle{ \int \frac{du}{u^4}}}. \end{equation*}

Evaluating the integral, we get

\begin{align*} \displaystyle{\frac{1}{3}\int \frac{du}{u^4}}\amp = \displaystyle{ \frac{1}{3}\int u^{-4}du}\\ \amp = -\frac{1}{9}u^{-3}+C. \end{align*}

Putting the answer back in terms of \(t\text{,}\) we get

\begin{align*} \displaystyle{ \int \frac{t^2+1}{(t^3+3t+1)^4}dt}\amp =-\frac{1}{9}u^{-3}+C\\ \amp =-\frac{1}{9(t^3+3t+1)^3}+C. \end{align*}
