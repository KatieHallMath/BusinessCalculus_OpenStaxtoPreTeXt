The horizontal scaling of a function occurs if we multiply the inputs \(x\) by the same positive constant. For \(c\gt 0 ,\) the graph of the function \(f(cx)\) is the graph of \(f(x)\) scaled horizontally by a factor of \(c.\) If \(c\gt 1 ,\) the graph of \(f(cx)\) is the graph of \(f(x)\) compressed horizontally. If \(0 \lt c\lt 1 ,\) the graph of \(f(cx)\) is the graph of \(f(x)\) stretched horizontally. For example, consider the function \(f(x)=\sqrt{ 2 x}\) and evaluate \(f\) at \(x / 2 .\) Since \(f(x / 2 )=\sqrt{x},\) the graph of \(f(x)=\sqrt{ 2 x}\) is the graph of \(y=\sqrt{x}\) compressed horizontally.
