To find the zeros of \(f,\) we need to factor the polynomial. First, when we factor \(x\) out of all the terms, we find

\begin{equation*} f(x)=x(x^2 − 3 x− 4 ). \end{equation*}

Then, when we factor the quadratic function \(x^2 − 3 x− 4 ,\) we find

\begin{equation*} f(x)=x(x− 4 )(x+ 1 ). \end{equation*}

Therefore, the zeros of \(f\) are \(x= 0 , 4 , −1 .\)
