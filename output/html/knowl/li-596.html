Item a.
The highest power of \(x\) in the denominator is \(x.\) Therefore, dividing the numerator and denominator by \(x\) and applying the algebraic limit laws, we see that
\begin{align*} \displaystyle{\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}\frac{3x-1}{2x+5}\amp =\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}\frac{3-1/x}{2+5/x}}\\ \amp=\displaystyle{\frac{\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}(3-1/x)}{\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}(2+5/x)}}\\ \amp=\displaystyle{\frac{\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}3-\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}1/x}{\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}2+\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}5/x}}\\ =\frac{3-0}{2+0}=\frac{3}{2}. \end{align*}
Since \(\displaystyle{\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}f(x)=\frac{3}{2}},\) we know that \(y=\frac{3}{2}\) is a horizontal asymptote for this function as shown in the following graph.
