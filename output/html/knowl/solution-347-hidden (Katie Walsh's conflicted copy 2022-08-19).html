Let \(u=2x+1.\) Then, \(du=2dx.\) To adjust the limits of integration, we note that when \(x=A,\;u=2A+1,\) and when \(x=4,\;u=9.\) So our substitution gives

\begin{align*} \displaystyle{ \int_{A}^{4} \frac{1}{2x+1}}\amp =\frac{1}{2}\displaystyle{ \int_{2A+1}^{9} \frac{1}{u}du}\\ \amp =\left.\frac{1}{2}\ln|u|\right|_{2A+1}^9\\ \amp =\frac{\ln 9-\ln|2A+1|}{2}.\\ \amp =\frac{\ln 9-\ln(2A+1)}{2}\text{ since }0\lt A\lt 4. \end{align*}
