A picture can sometimes tell us more about a function than the results of computations. Comparing functions by their graphs as well as by their algebraic expressions can often give new insight into the process of integration. Intuitively, we might say that if a function \(f(x)\) is above another function \(g(x),\) then the area between \(f(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis is greater than the area between \(g(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis. This is true depending on the interval over which the comparison is made.
