Item c.
Dividing the numerator and denominator by \(x,\) we have
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to\pm\infty}\frac{3x^2+4x}{x+2}=\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}\frac{3x+4}{1+2/x}. \end{equation*}
As \(x\to\pm\infty,\) the denominator approaches \(1.\) As \(x\to\infty,\) the numerator approaches \(+\infty.\) As \(x\to-\infty,\) the numerator approaches \(-\infty.\) Therefore \(\displaystyle{\lim_{x\to\infty}f(x)=\infty},\) whereas \(\displaystyle{\lim_{x\to-\infty}f(x)=-\infty}\) as shown in the following figure.
