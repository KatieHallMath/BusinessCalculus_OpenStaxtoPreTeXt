Solution 5.23.1.

With \(n=4\) over the interval \([1,2],\Delta x =\frac{1}{4}.\) We can list the intervals as \([1,1.25],[1.25,1.5],[1.5,1.75],[1.75,2].\) Because the function is decreasing over the interval \([1,2],\) Figure 5.24 shows that a lower sum is obtained by using the right endpoints.

The Riemann sum is

\begin{align*} \displaystyle{ \sum_{k=1}^4(10-x^2)}{(0.25)}\amp =0.25[10-(1.25)^2+10-(1.5)^2+10-(1.75)^2+10-(2)^2\\ \amp =0.25[8.4375+7.75+6.9375+6]\\ \amp =7.28. \end{align*}

The area of 7.28 is a lower sum and an underestimate.
