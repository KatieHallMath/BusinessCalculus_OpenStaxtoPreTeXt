We look at some examples shortly. But, before we do, let’s take a moment and talk about some specific choices for \({x_i^* }.\) Although any choice for \({x_i^* }\) gives us an estimate of the area under the curve, we don’t necessarily know whether that estimate is too high (overestimate) or too low (underestimate). If it is important to know whether our estimate is high or low, we can select our value for \({x_i^* }\) to guarantee one result or the other.
