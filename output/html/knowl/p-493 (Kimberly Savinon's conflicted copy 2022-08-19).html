Limit from the right: Let \(f(x)\) be a function defined at all values in an open interval of the form \((a,c),\) and let \(L \) be a real number. If the values of the function \(f(x)\) approach the real number L as the values of \(x\) (where \(x\gt a .\)) approach the number \(a \text{,}\) then we say that \(L \) is the limit of \(f(x)\) as \(x\) approaches \(a \) from the right.
