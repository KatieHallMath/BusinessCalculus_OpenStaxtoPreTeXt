The end behavior for rational functions and functions involving radicals is a little more complicated than for polynomials. In ExampleÂ 4.106, we show that the limits at infinity of a rational function \(f(x)=\frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\) depend on the relationship between the degree of the numerator and the degree of the denominator. To evaluate the limits at infinity for a rational function, we divide the numerator and denominator by the highest power of \(x\) appearing in the denominator. This determines which term in the overall expression dominates the behavior of the function at large values of \(x.\)
