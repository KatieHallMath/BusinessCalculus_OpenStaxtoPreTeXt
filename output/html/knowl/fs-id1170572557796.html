Theorem 2.68. Limits of Polynomial and Rational Functions.

Let \(p(x)\) and \(q(x)\) be polynomial functions. Let \(a\) be a real number. Then,

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \to a }p(x)=p(a) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \to a }\frac{p(x)}{q(x)}=\frac{p(a)}{q(a)} \text{ when } q(a)\neq 0. \end{equation*}
