Solution 5.97.1.

Use substitution. Let \(u=x^4+3x^2\text{,}\) then \(du=4x^3+6x\text{.}\) Alter \(du\) by factoring out the 2. Thus,

\begin{align*} du\amp=(4x^3+6x)dx\\ \amp=2(2x^3+3x)dx\\ \frac{1}{2}\amp=(2x^3+3x)dx \end{align*}

Rewite the integrand in \(u\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \int\frac{2x^3+3x}{x^4+3x^2}dx=\frac{1}{2}\int u^{-1}du. \end{equation*}
Then we have
\begin{align*} \frac{1}{2}\int u^{-1}du\amp=\frac{1}{2}\ln|u|+C\\ \amp=\frac{1}{2}\ln|x^4+3x^2|+C. \end{align*}
