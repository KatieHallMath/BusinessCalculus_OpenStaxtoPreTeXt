It is important to understand that when we write statements such as \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x)=+\infty\) or \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x)=-\infty\) we are describing the behavior of the function, as we have just defined it. We are not asserting that a limit exists. For the limit of a function \(f(x)\) to exist at \(a \text{,}\) it must approach a real number \(L \) as \(x\) approaches \(a \text{.}\) That said, if, for example, \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x)=+\infty,\) we always write \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x)=+\infty\) rather than \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x)\) DNE.
