From this theorem, we can evaluate any integral involving a sum, difference, or constant multiple of functions with antiderivatives that are known. Evaluating integrals involving products, quotients, or compositions is more complicated (see Example 4.165b. for an example involving an antiderivative of a product.) We look at and address integrals involving these more complicated functions in Introduction to Integration 5.
