Solution 1.132.1.

  1. If \(P\) dollars are invested in an account at an annual interest rate \(r,\) compounded continuously, then \(A(t)=Pe^{rt}.\) Here \(P=.500\) and \(r=0.055.\) Therefore, \(A(t)=500e^{0.055t}.\)

  2. After \(10\) years, the amount of money in the account is

    \begin{equation*} A(10)=500e^{0.055\cdot 10}=500e^{0.55}≈.866.63. \end{equation*}
    After \(20\) years, the amount of money in the account is
    \begin{equation*} A(20)=500e^{0.055\cdot 20}=500e^{1.1}≈.1,502.08. \end{equation*}
