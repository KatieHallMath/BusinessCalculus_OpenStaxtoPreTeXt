Let \(f\text{,}\) \(g\text{,}\) \(u\text{,}\) and \(F\) be as specified in the theorem. Then

\begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx}F(g(x))\amp =F'(g(x))g'(x)\\ \amp =f[g(x)]g'(x). \end{align*}

Integrating both sides with respect to \(x\text{,}\) we see that

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle{ \int f[g(x)]g'(x)dx}=F(g(x))+C. \end{equation*}

If we now substitute \(u=g(x),\) and \(du=g'(x)dx,\) we get

\begin{align*} \displaystyle{ \int f[g(x)]g'(x)dx}\amp =\displaystyle{ \int f}(u)du\\ \amp =F(u)+C\\ \amp =F(g(x))+C. \end{align*}
