Consider the function \(f(x)=|x+ 3 |\) and evaluate this function at \(x− 3 .\) Since \(f(x− 3 )=|x|\) and \(x− 3 \lt x,\) the graph of \(f(x)=|x+ 3 |\) is the graph of \(y=|x|\) shifted left 3 units. Similarly, the graph of \(f(x)=|x− 3 |\) is the graph of \(y=|x|\) shifted right \(3 \) units (Figure 1.73).
