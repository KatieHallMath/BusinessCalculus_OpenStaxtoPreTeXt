We first need to compute where the graphs of the functions intersect. Setting \(f(x)=g(x),\) we get

\begin{align*} f(x)\amp =g(x)\\ 9-\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)^2\amp = 6-x\\ 9-\frac{x^2}{4}\amp = 6-x\\ 36-x^2\amp = 24-4x\\ x^2-4x-12\amp = 0\\ (x-6)(x+2)\amp =0. \end{align*}

The graphs of the functions intersect when \(x=6\) or \(x=-2,\) so we want to integrate from \(-2\) to \(6.\) Since \(f(x)\geq g(x)\) for \(-2\leq x\leq 6,\) we obtain

\begin{align*} A\amp =\displaystyle{ \int_{a}^{b} [f(x)-g(x)]dx}\\ \amp=\displaystyle{\int_{-2}^{6}\left[9-(\frac{x}{2})^2-(6-x)\right]dx}=\displaystyle{\int_{-2}^{6}\left[3-\frac{x^2}{4}+x\right]dx}\\ \amp=\left.\left[3x-\frac{x^3}{12}+\frac{x^2}{2}\right] \right|_{-2}^{6}=\frac{64}{3}. \end{align*}

The area of the region is \(64/3\) units \(^2\text{.}\)
