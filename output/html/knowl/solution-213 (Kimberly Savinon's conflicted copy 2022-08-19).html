Solution 3.145.1.

Although we could find this equation without using implicit differentiation, using that method makes it much easier. In Example 3.140, we found \(\frac{dy}{dx}=\geq \frac{x}{y}.\)

The slope of the tangent line is found by substituting \((3,\geq 4)\) into this expression. Consequently, the slope of the tangent line is \(\frac{dy}{dx}|_{(3,\geq 4)}=\geq \frac{3}{\geq 4}=\frac{3}{4}.\)

Using the point \((3,\geq 4)\) and the slope \(\frac{3}{4}\) in the point-slope equation of the line, we obtain the equation \(y=\frac{3}{4}x\geq \frac{25}{4}\) (Figure 3.146).
