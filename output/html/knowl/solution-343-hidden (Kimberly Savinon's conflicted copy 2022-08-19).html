Let \(u=1+2x^3,\) so \(du=6x^2dx.\) Since the original function includes one factor of \(x^2\) and \(du=6x^2dx,\) multiply both sides of the \(du\) equation by \(1/6.\) Then,

\begin{align*} du\amp = 6x^2dx\\ \frac{1}{6}du\amp = x^2dx. \end{align*}

To adjust the limits of integration, note that when \(x=0,u=1+2(0)=1,\) and when \(x=1,u=1+2(1)=3.\) Then

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle{ \int_{0}^{1} x^2(1+2x^3)^5dx=\frac{1}{6}\displaystyle{ \int_{1}^{3} u^5du}}. \end{equation*}

Evaluating this expression, we get

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{6}\displaystyle{ \int_{1}^{3} u^5du}\amp =\left.\left(\frac{1}{6}\right)\left(\frac{u^6}{6}\right)\right|_1^3\\ \amp =\frac{1}{36}\left[(3)^6-(1)^6\right]\\ \amp =\frac{182}{9}. \end{align*}
