Step 1. The derivative is \(f'(x)=3x^2-6x-9.\) To find the critical points, we need to find where \(f'(x)=0.\) Factoring the polynomial, we conclude that the critical points must satisfy

\begin{equation*} 3(x^2-2x-3)=3(x-3)(x+1)=0. \end{equation*}

Therefore, the critical points are \(x=3,-1.\) Now divide the interval \((-\infty,\infty)\) into the smaller intervals \((-\infty,-1),(-1,3) \text{ and } (3,\infty).\)

Step 2. Since \(f\) is a continuous function, to determine the sign of \(f'(x)\) over each subinterval, it suffices to choose a point over each of the intervals \((-\infty,-1),(-1,3) \text{ and } (3,\infty)\) and determine the sign of \(f'\) at each of these points. For example, let’s choose \(x=-2,x=0, \text{ and } x=4\) as test points.
