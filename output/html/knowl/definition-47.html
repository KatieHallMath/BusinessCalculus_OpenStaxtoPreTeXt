Definition 5.38.

Let \(f(x)\) be an integrable function defined on an interval \([a,b].\) Let \(A_{1}\) represent the area between \(f(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis that lies above the axis and let \(A_{2}\) represent the area between \(f(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis that lies below the axis. Then, the net signed area between \(f(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis is given by

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle{\int_{a}^{b} f(x)dx} =A_1-A_2. \end{equation*}

The total area between \(f(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis is given by

\begin{equation*} \int_{a}^{b}|f(x)|dx=A_1+A_2. \end{equation*}
