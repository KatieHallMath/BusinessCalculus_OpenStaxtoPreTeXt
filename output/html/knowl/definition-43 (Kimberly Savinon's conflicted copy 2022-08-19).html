Definition 5.4.

A set of points \(P={x_i}\) for \(i=0,1,2 , \dots , n\) with \(a=x_0\lt x_1\lt x_2\lt \cdots \lt x_n=b,\) which divides the interval \([a,b]\) into subintervals of the form \([x_0,x_1],[x_1,x_2] , \dots , [x_{n-1} ,x_n]\) is called a partition of \([a,b].\) If the subintervals all have the same width, the set of points forms a regular partition of the interval \([a,b].\)
