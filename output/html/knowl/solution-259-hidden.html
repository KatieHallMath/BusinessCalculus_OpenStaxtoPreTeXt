1. Since the coefficient of \(x^3\) is \(-5,\) the graph of \(f(x)=-5x^3\) involves a vertical stretch and reflection of the graph of \(y=x^3\) about the \(x\)-axis. Therefore, \(\displaystyle{\lim_{x\to\infty}(-5x^3)=-\infty}\) and \(\displaystyle{\lim_{x\to-\infty}(-5x^3)=\infty.}\)

  2. Since the coefficient of \(x^4\) is \(2,\) the graph of \(f(x)=2x^4\) is a vertical stretch of the graph of \(y=x^4.\) Therefore, \(\displaystyle{\lim_{x\to\infty}2x^4=\infty}\) and \(\displaystyle{\lim_{x\to-\infty}2x^4=\infty.}\)
