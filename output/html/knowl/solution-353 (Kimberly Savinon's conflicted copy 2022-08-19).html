Solution 6.15.1.

Let's first find where the graphs intersect.

\begin{align*} f(x)\amp=g(x)\\ -x\amp=2-x^2\\ x^2-x-2\amp=0\\ (x-2)(x+1)\amp=0\\ x\amp=-1,\;2 \end{align*}

The graphs of the functions intersect at \(x=-1\text{ and }x=2.\) The region is depicted in the following figure.

For \(x\in[-2,-1],\) \(f(x)\geq g(x)\text{.}\) On the other hand, for \(x\in[-1,2],\) \(g(x)\geq f(x)\text{.}\) Then,

\begin{align*} A\amp =\displaystyle{ \int_{a}^{b} |f(x)-g(x)|dx}\\ \amp=\displaystyle{ \int_{-2}^{2}|-x-(2-x^2)|dx}=\displaystyle{ \int_{-2}^{-1}[-x-(2-x^2)]dx}+\displaystyle{ \int_{-1}^{2}[(2-x^2)-(-x)]dx}\\ \amp=\displaystyle{ \int_{-2}^{-1}(x^2-x-2)dx}+\displaystyle{ \int_{-1}^{2}(-x^2+x+2)dx}\\ \amp=\left.\left[\frac{1}{3}x^3-\frac{1}{2}x^2-2x\right] \right|_{-2}^{-1}+\left.\left[-\frac{1}{3}x^3+\frac{1}{2}x^2+2x\right] \right|_{-1}^{2}\\ \amp=\left(\frac{7}{6}-\left(-\frac{2}{3}\right)\right)+\left(\frac{10}{3}-\left(-\frac{7}{6}\right)\right)=\frac{19}{3}. \end{align*}

The area of the region is \(\frac{19}{3}\) units \(^2\text{.}\)
