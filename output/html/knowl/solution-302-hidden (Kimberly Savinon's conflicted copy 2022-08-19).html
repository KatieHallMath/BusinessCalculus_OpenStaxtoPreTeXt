Calculate the \(x\)-intercept as \((2,0)\) (set \(y=0,\) solve for \(x\)). To find the total area, take the area below the \(x\)-axis over the subinterval \([0,2]\) and add it to the area above the \(x\)-axis on the subinterval \([2,6]\) (FigureÂ 5.40).

We have

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle{\int_{0}^{6} |(x-2)|dx} =A_2+A_1. \end{equation*}

Then, using the formula for the area of a triangle, we obtain

\begin{equation*} A_2=\frac{1}{2}bh=\frac{1}{2}\cdot 2\cdot 2=2 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} A_1=\frac{1}{2}bh=\frac{1}{2}\cdot 4\cdot 4=8. \end{equation*}

The total area, then, is

\begin{equation*} A_1+A_2=8+2=10. \end{equation*}
