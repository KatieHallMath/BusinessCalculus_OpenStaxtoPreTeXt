Theorem 3.70. Product Rule.

Let \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) be differentiable functions. Then

\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx}(f(x)g(x))=\frac{d}{dx}(f(x))\cdot g(x)+\frac{d}{dx}(g(x))\cdot f(x). \end{equation*}

That is,

\begin{equation*} \text{ if } j(x)=f(x)g(x),\text{ then } j'(x)=f'(x)g(x)+g'(x)f(x). \end{equation*}

This means that the derivative of a product of two functions is the derivative of the first function times the second function plus the derivative of the second function times the first function.
