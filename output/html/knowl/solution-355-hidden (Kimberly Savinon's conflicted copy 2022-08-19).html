The graphs of the functions intersect at \(x=1\) (set \(f(x)=g(x)\) and solve for \(x\)), so we evaluate two separate integrals: one over the interval \([0,1]\) and one over the interval \([1,2].\)

Over the interval \([0,1],\) the region is bounded above by \(f(x)=x^2\) and below by the \(x\)-axis, so we have

\begin{equation*} A_1=\displaystyle{ \int_{0}^{1} x^2dx}=\left.\frac{x^3}{3}\right|_0^1=\frac{1}{3}. \end{equation*}

Over the interval \([1,2],\) the region is bounded above by \(g(x)=2-x\) and below by the \(x\text{ -axis, }\) so we have

\begin{equation*} A_2=\displaystyle{ \int_{1}^{2} (2-x)dx}=\left.\left[2x-\frac{x^2}{2}\right]\right|_1^2=\frac{1}{2}. \end{equation*}

Adding these areas together, we obtain

\begin{equation*} A=A_1+A_2=\frac{1}{3}+\frac{1}{2}=\frac{5}{6}. \end{equation*}

The area of the region is \(5/6\) units \(^2\text{.}\)
