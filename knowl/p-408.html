As \(t \) is chosen closer to \(a\text{,}\) the average velocity becomes closer to the instantaneous velocity. Note that finding the average velocity of a position function over a time interval is essentially the same as finding the slope of a secant line to a function. Furthermore, to find the slope of a tangent line at a point \(a\text{,}\) we let the \(x\)-values approach \(a\) in the slope of the secant line. Similarly, to find the instantaneous velocity at time \(a\text{,}\) we let the \(t \)-values approach \(a\) in the average velocity.
