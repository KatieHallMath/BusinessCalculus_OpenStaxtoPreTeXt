At this point we can indeed conclude that \(\lim_{x\to0}\sin (1/x)\) does not exist. (Mathematicians frequently abbreviate “does not exist” as DNE. Thus, we would write \(\lim_{x\to0}\sin (1/x)\) DNE.) The graph of \(f(x)=\sin (1/x)\) is shown in Figure 2.38 and it gives a clearer picture of the behavior of \(\sin(1/x)\) as \(x\) approaches 0. You can see that \(\sin(1/x)\) oscillates ever more wildly between -1 and 1 as \(x\) approaches 0.
