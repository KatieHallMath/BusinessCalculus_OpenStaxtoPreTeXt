Since \(e\gt 1,\) we know \(e^x\) is increasing on \((.\infty,\infty).\) In FigureÂ 1.131, we show a graph of \(f(x)=e^x\) along with a tangent line to the graph of at \(x=0.\) We give a precise definition of tangent line in the next chapter; but, informally, we say a tangent line to a graph of \(f\) at \(x=a\) is a line that passes through the point \((a,f(a))\) and has the same "slope" as \(f\) at that point \(.\) The function \(f(x)=e^x\) is the only exponential function \(b^x\) with tangent line at \(x=0\) that has a slope of 1. As we see later in the text, having this property makes the natural exponential function the most simple exponential function to use in many instances.
