Solution 1.84.1.
Since our operating costs (fixed costs) are $200 per week and the marginal cost is $2 per ice cream sundae, then
\begin{equation*} C(x)=2x+200\text{.} \end{equation*}
Since the selling price is $6 per ice cream sundae, then
\begin{equation*} R(x)=6x\text{.} \end{equation*}
Using \(C(x)=2x+200\) and \(R(x)=6x\text{,}\) we can obtain the profit function.
\begin{equation*} P(x)=R(x)-C(x) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} P(x)=6x-(2x+200) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} P(x)=6x-2x-200 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} P(x)=4x-200 \end{equation*}

To find the break-even quantity, we may proceed by using two different methods. Both yield the same solution.

Method 1:\(\;R(x)=C(x)\)
\begin{equation*} 6x=2x+200 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 4x=200 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} x=50 \end{equation*}
Method 2:\(\;P(x)=0\)
\begin{equation*} 4x-200=0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 4x=200 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} x=50 \end{equation*}
Peter must sell at least 50 ice cream sundaes in one week in order to break-even.
