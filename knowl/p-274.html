Let’s consider the relationship between the graph of a function \(f\) and the graph of its inverse. Consider the graph of \(f\) shown in Figure 1.106 and a point \((a,b)\) on the graph. Since \(b=f(a),\) then \(f^{−1} (b)=a.\) Therefore, when we graph \(f^{−1} ,\) the point \((b,a)\) is on the graph. As a result, the graph of \(f^{−1} \) is a reflection of the graph of \(f\) about the line \(y=x.\)
